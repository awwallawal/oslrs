Livestock Value Chain Strategy for FFS

Policy Workshop & Stakeholder Briefing | January 2026

Executive Summary

This strategy outlines a comprehensive approach to integrating livestock value chain perspectives into Nigeria's Farmers' Field School (FFS) program. The goal is to transform subsistence-oriented livestock production into ICT-innovative, knowledge-driven, market-oriented, climate-resilient, inclusive, and profitable enterprises.

Current Challenges

  • Low productivity and high mortality rates
  • Weak input and service delivery systems
  • Poor husbandry and biosecurity practices
  • Limited access to finance and markets
  • High post-production losses and food safety risks
  • Climate and disease shocks (ND, ASF, PPR, CBPP)

Value Chain Framework within FFS

1. Research-Extension Linkage → 2. Input Supply → 3. Production & Husbandry → 4. Animal Health → 5. Aggregation & Processing → 6. Marketing → 7. Support Services

Six Strategic Pillars with Targets

Pillar Key FFS Strategies Target Outcomes
1. Input Supply & Services Input quality testing, local feed formulation, bulk purchasing, vet engagement 20% cost reduction 10% quality improvement
2. Production & GAHP Season-long learning, comparative trials, farmer-led data collection 20% productivity increase 10% mortality reduction
3. Animal Health & Biosecurity Disease surveillance, biosecurity mapping, vaccination calendars, AMR training 20% disease reduction 20% food safety improvement
4. Aggregation & Value Addition Collective aggregation, basic processing, women/youth micro-processing units 30% margin increase 20% loss reduction
5. Marketing & Business Market mapping, standards training, collective marketing, digital tools 20% market access improvement 10% commercial transition
6. Finance & Institutions Financial literacy, microfinance linkages, insurance, government engagement 20% investment increase 10% institutional support

Cross-Cutting Themes

Gender & Social Inclusion

  • Targeted women-only and youth FFS groups
  • Enterprises with low entry barriers
  • Leadership and entrepreneurship training

Climate-Smart Practices

  • Heat stress management
  • Improved fodder, hay, silage systems
  • Manure management and biogas

Nutrition & Food Safety

  • Animal-source foods promotion
  • Hygiene and slaughter practices
  • Milk handling safety protocols

Alignment with National Policies

L-PRES ($500M World Bank Project)

  • 1.4 million beneficiaries targeted
  • Focus on beef cattle, sheep & goat value chains
  • Crisis prevention and conflict mitigation
  • Climate adaptation integration with NiMet

NLTP (National Transformation Plan)

  • ₦100 billion budget (80% Federal, 20% States)
  • 2 million+ jobs target by 2028
  • Modernizing through ranching and dairy processing
  • Farmer-herder conflict resolution

FFS Success Stories from Africa

Tanzania - AMR in Poultry

FAO integrated behavioural science into FFS to address antimicrobial resistance in broiler production across the region.

Zimbabwe - Broiler Production

FAO piloted FFS in 8 districts, graduating 106 farmers and facilitators as multipliers.

Kenya - Agropastoralist Schools

Built drought resilience through fodder production; FFS now institutionalized in national extension policy.

Eastern Africa Network

2,000+ active FFS with 50,000+ direct beneficiaries; farmers building bottom-up producer organizations.

Implementation & Sustainability

Key Partners

  • Lead: FMLD & State Ministries
  • Technical: Research institutes, universities, NGOs
  • Delivery: Extension officers as FFS facilitators
  • Private Sector: Input suppliers, processors, off-takers

Scaling Strategy

  • FFS groups graduate into cooperatives/agribusiness clusters
  • Community-Based Facilitators (CBFs) for local ownership
  • Integration into state livestock development plans
  • Public-Private Partnership models for sustainable funding
Timeline: FFS cycles run 9-12 months aligned with livestock production cycles. Phases include: Facilitator Training (Months 1-3) → FFS Establishment (Months 3-6) → Learning Cycles (Months 6-12) → Graduation & Scaling (Month 12+)

Monitoring & Evaluation Indicators

Productivity Mortality rates, growth rates, output per animal
Economic Income levels, profitability, cost reduction
Adoption GAHP and biosecurity practice uptake rates
Market Collective sales, market linkages, price realization
