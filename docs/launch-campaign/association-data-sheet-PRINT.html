For the Association Head — read first

This one page is what makes the sheet come back. Keep it with the sheet.

1 · Read this to each member before you write their Consent

English: "The Oyo State Government is building a Skills Registry. If you agree, your details will be stored and used to contact you about skills, training, jobs, and economic opportunities for your trade and area. You can ask to be removed at any time. I will write Yes in the Consent column if you agree."

Yoruba: [AWWAL / PAIGE: insert Yoruba translation of the declaration above — and Yoruba sub-labels under the column headers if printing bilingual]

• If the member does not agree (says No, or won't answer) — do not enter them. Leave them off the sheet.
• You are collecting other people's personal details on their behalf — only write down members who have heard this and agreed.

2 · How to fill the sheet

  1. One row per member. Use BLOCK CAPITALS, black ink. Row 0 (italic) is a worked example — copy its style.
  2. Phone is compulsory — it is how we avoid entering someone twice and how the member is contacted. No phone = cannot enter the member.
  3. Trade: pick the closest from the boxed list. If none fits, write it under "Other" — don't invent spellings (e.g. always "Tailoring/Fashion", never also "tailor" / "fashion designer").
  4. LGA: must be one of the 33 in the box — where the member works or lives.
  5. Date of birth if known; if not, write their age. NIN only if they have it to hand — blank is fine.
  6. Fill the header block once (your association name, your name + phone, primary LGA, date, and the total declared member count).

3 · How & when to return it

Send a clear photo or the file of every completed sheet to:
  Return to: [AWWAL: named contact + phone/WhatsApp]   By: [AWWAL: deadline date]
That one named person chases and reconciles. Your declared member count tells us how many entries to expect from you — so under-delivery is visible and we will follow up.
