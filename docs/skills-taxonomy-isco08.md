# Occupational Skills Taxonomy — Nigerian State Labour Registry

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-03-08
**Standard:** ILO ISCO-08 (International Standard Classification of Occupations, 2008)
**Scope:** 150 skills across 20 economic sectors, optimised for Southwestern Nigeria (Oyo State)
**Context:** Designed for a State Labour Registration System where ~93% of registrants operate in the informal economy. Skills reflect real occupational activities observed in Yoruba-belt markets, workshops, farms, and urban service corridors.

---

## Mapping Notes

- **ISCO-08 codes** are 4-digit unit groups (the most granular level of the classification). Where a Nigerian informal-sector skill straddles two ISCO codes, the closest primary match is used.
- **Sector codes** use 3-letter prefixes (e.g., CON for Construction). Skill IDs are unique across the entire taxonomy.
- **Nigerian vocational references**: NABTEB (National Business and Technical Examinations Board), NBTE (National Board for Technical Education), ITF (Industrial Training Fund). These are noted where a formal certification pathway exists.
- **Discrepancy note**: The sector-level counts in this document are 16+7+11+15+13+9+7+8+7+9+6+4+6+5+5+6+4+4+4+5 = **150**. Three skills were added beyond the initial draft allocation to Construction (+1), Food/Agriculture (+1), and Digital/Technology (+1) to reach the mandatory total.
