# OSLSR — Hosting Guide for the Permanent Secretary

### BERAP 2025 Reform Area 1 · Action Step i — Stakeholder Engagement with Private Sector

**The room:** representatives of Manufacturing Association of Nigeria (MAN), Association of Nigerian Artisans and Technicians (ASNAT), Association of Skilled and Vocational Artisans of Nigeria (ASVAN) — the three BERAP-named private-sector contributors. Plus contributing MDA representatives where present.

**Your role:** host the formal BERAP Action Step i. Open the floor, frame the partnership, secure concrete next-step commitments before the room leaves.

---

## The 30-second opening

> "Welcome. Today is the formal Stakeholder Engagement step under BERAP 2025 Reform Area 1 — the State Electronic Labour Register. The Oyo State Labour & Skills Registry is built and ready. Statewide field enumeration begins late May 2026. The marketplace opens in Q3 2026. Today is where you, as the BERAP-named partners representing skilled labour and industry in Oyo State, help us shape how this asset serves your members and the businesses that need them."

---

## What we are asking of MAN, ASNAT, ASVAN today

Three concrete asks. Each is a partnership commitment, not a one-time signature.

1. **Trade taxonomy validation.** The registry classifies workers by trade (welders, masons, electricians, tailors, mechanics, etc.). We need each association to validate the trade list for completeness within their domain — and to commit a named focal point for ongoing review.
2. **Member mobilisation.** Each association has a membership reach we cannot match. We are asking each to nominate one or two pilot LGAs where their members will be the first wave of registrants, alongside the State's enumerators. This becomes the proof of concept the rest of the State sees.
3. **Marketplace input.** When the marketplace opens to employers in Q3 2026, your members will be on the supply side and many of your member-businesses on the demand side. We want association input on how matches are surfaced, how skill levels are presented, and how the verification badge works. A small joint design group, three sessions, before Q3.

## Anticipated questions from the room

### "What does this mean for our members?"

> Free registration. A verified, portable record of skills owned by the worker, recognised by the State. Discovery by serious employers without paying gatekeepers. For your associations, it means your members are findable, comparable, and can prove their skills when they bid for work.

### "How is this different from the federal SUPA / ITF?"

> SUPA and ITF operate **nationally** and focus on apprenticeships and training placements. OSLSR is **state-level** and focuses on registry and discoverability — Oyo's authoritative answer to *"who can do what work, where, in this State."* The two are complementary: OSLSR can ingest federal artisan data with clear source labelling, and we can share aggregate views with federal partners under formal agreements. We are filling the state-level gap, not duplicating federal effort.

### "Who owns the data?"

> The State of Oyo owns the registry. The Ministry of Trade, Industry & Cooperatives is the operational custodian. Your members own the rights to their own profile data and can update or withdraw it. Partner access — including yours, when we agree on it — happens under signed Data Sharing Agreements. Ownership does not transfer with access.

### "Will our members' data be commercialised behind our backs?"

> No. The Data Sharing Agreement framework is explicit: every external use of the registry is logged, scoped, and traceable to the consenting party. There is no quiet sale of member data to third parties. The marketplace itself is a public good — free for workers, free for individual employer searches.

### "Will employers pay to use the marketplace?"

> The public marketplace is free — workers and individual employers both. Premium institutional services — bulk verification APIs for large recruiters, anonymised analytics for industry research — may be introduced under formal agreements, with revenue to the State. The default position is **free access for the workforce, regulated access for institutional users**.

### "Is this NDPA compliant?"

> Yes. Identity is verified using NIN only — no BVN collection. Data is encrypted at rest and in transit. Every access is logged in an immutable audit trail. Workers consent explicitly, can review what they have shared, and the privacy notice is published at oyoskills.com. Aligned with the Nigeria Data Protection Act.

### "What about workers in low-connectivity LGAs?"

> The field application works **offline for up to 7 days**. Enumerators can collect in any LGA regardless of internet, and the system synchronises automatically when connectivity returns. This is what makes statewide coverage realistic.

### "Can we badge the registry / co-brand?"

> The State holds the master brand. Co-recognition is on the table — for example, a "Validated by ASNAT" badge on a member's profile, or association logos on the partners page of oyoskills.com. The shape of co-recognition is part of what we want to agree before Q3 launch.

### "What is the next milestone?"

> **Late May 2026** — statewide field enumeration begins. **Q3 2026** — marketplace formally opens. **October 2026** — operational handover from the Build team to the Ministry. Today's outcomes feed directly into the field phase, which starts in days.

## What we are committing to in return

Three commitments back to the partners. Make these explicit.

1. **A formal seat at the table.** A named association representative for each body, invited to the quarterly Registry Governance review (first meeting Q3 2026, before marketplace launch).
2. **Member visibility on oyoskills.com.** A partners page acknowledging MAN, ASNAT, ASVAN as BERAP-named contributors, with linkbacks to your sites — live within 30 days of today's session.
3. **Co-design on the marketplace.** Three working sessions with each association before Q3 2026, on trade taxonomy, skill-level presentation, and verification badge design.

---

## Concrete close — what to leave the room with

Before the room disperses, the PS should secure four named outputs:

1. **Focal point per association** — name + email + phone for ongoing contact (not the chair, the working contact)
2. **Pilot LGA nomination per association** — one or two LGAs where their members will lead the first wave
3. **Calendar for the trade-taxonomy review** — when, with whom, in what format
4. **Date for the marketplace co-design kickoff** — Q3 2026 is the deadline, working back from there

## What NOT to commit to in the room

- Do **not** name a marketplace pricing model for institutional users beyond "free for workforce, regulated for institutions." Pricing is a Q3 decision, not a today decision.
- Do **not** promise data exclusivity to any single association. Equal access framework applies.
- Do **not** commit specific numbers from the registry yet. Field phase has not begun. First publishable numbers come at end of June 2026 at the earliest.
- Do **not** agree to badge changes or domain changes on the spot. Brand decisions need the Ministry's design review first.
