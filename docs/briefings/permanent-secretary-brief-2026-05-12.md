# Oyo State Labour & Skills Registry

### A State Electronic Labour Register, under the Oyo State Business Enabling Reforms Action Plan (BERAP) 2025

| 33 LGAs | 165 government staff | 100,000+ workers (Phase 1 target) | Statewide coverage |
|---|---|---|---|

---

**The Oyo State Labour & Skills Registry (OSLSR)** is a government-built digital register of skilled, semi-skilled, and unskilled workers across all 33 Local Government Areas of Oyo State. It is the State's first authoritative source of who can do what work, where, and to what standard — collected directly from residents, owned by the State, and shared with employers, investors, and partner agencies under formal agreements.

---

### Why Oyo State is building it (BERAP Reform Objectives)

- **Connect employers to skilled workers** — corporate organisations and businesses can find verified skilled labour without guesswork.
- **Strengthen the position of skilled workers** — artisans, traders, and professionals gain a recognised, portable record of their skills.
- **Attract investment to Oyo State** — a documented workforce tells investors what the State can deliver, on the ground, today.

**BERAP key targets:** improve State ranking on skilled-labour access · reduce unemployment by 3% · grow State revenue.

---

### Who benefits (BERAP beneficiary groups)

- **Skilled Labours** — welders, masons, carpenters, traders, electricians, plumbers, tailors, mechanics, and every other trade. Free registration. Verified. Searchable.
- **Corporate Organisations & Investors** — find pre-verified workers by trade, location, and experience; assess the State's workforce capacity.
- **Researchers** — universities, policy institutes, and development partners access anonymised aggregate data for research and planning.
- **Government** — the Ministry, partner MDAs, and policy-makers gain a real-time, evidence-based picture of Oyo's labour market for targeted intervention.

---

### What is live today

- Public 5-step self-registration (mobile + web) — register from anywhere in ~3 minutes
- 165-strong field-enumeration capacity ready to deploy across 33 LGAs (offline-capable for low-connectivity areas)
- Skills marketplace (employers can search the verified registry)
- Government oversight dashboards + audit trails (NDPA-compliant, encrypted at rest and in transit)
- Two official domains live: **oyoskills.com** + **oyotradeministry.com.ng**

### What comes next

- **Late May 2026** — Statewide field enumeration begins
- **Q3 2026** — Skills marketplace formally opens to employers
- **October 2026** — Operational handover to the Ministry under the Transfer Protocol
