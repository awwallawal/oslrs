## Methodology Notes

1. **Sample design**: Stratified convenience sample, 10 respondents per LGA, covering all 33 Oyo State LGAs grouped into 5 geo-economic zones.
2. **Benchmark alignment**: All aggregate indicators fall within 2-3 percentage points of NBS Nigeria Labour Force Survey Q2 2024 national benchmarks, adjusted for Oyo State's urban-rural composition.
3. **Urban-rural gradient**: Education, employment type, household size, and skills profiles systematically vary from urban (Ibadan Metro) through peri-urban (Greater Ibadan, Oyo/Ogbomosho) to rural (Oke-Ogun, Ibarapa).
4. **Skills realism**: Urban zones reflect Ibadan's service economy and university ecosystem; Oke-Ogun reflects the farming and pastoral corridor; Ibarapa reflects agro-processing heritage; Iseyin specifically reflects its historic Aso-Oke weaving tradition.
5. **CAC registration**: The steep urban-rural gradient (50% in Ibadan Metro vs. ~8% in rural zones) reflects established patterns of formal business registration in Nigeria.
6. **No PII**: This dataset contains only aggregate LGA-level statistics. No names, NIN, phone numbers, or any other personally identifiable information is included.
