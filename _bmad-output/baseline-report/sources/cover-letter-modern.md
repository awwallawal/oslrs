## Subject: Pre-Field Survey Status Report (OSLSR)

Honourable Commissioner,

This letter accompanies the Pre-Field Survey Status Report for the Oyo State Labour & Skills Registry. The report sets out where the Registry stands ahead of fieldwork, which is scheduled to begin in the first week of May 2026.

## Where the programme stands

The Registry has progressed to a deployed, secured, and operational state:

- **The Registry is established and operational.** The platform supports five operational user roles (Enumerator, Clerk, Supervisor, Assessor, and Super Admin) plus a Public tier, offline-first operation for field conditions, and a four-layer data-quality assurance protocol.
- **The data centre is secured and equipped.** Operator access is restricted to a private overlay network, public traffic is protected at the edge, and audit logs are append-only and hash-chained to support the seven-year retention required by the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023.
- **The analytics layer is in place.** The Registry can produce descriptive statistics, inferential statistics, cross-tabulations, equity analyses, geographic visualisations, and PDF policy briefs against field-survey data without further engineering work.
- **The Registry platform is delivered to specification.** It is independently assessed at A- security posture (state-government-grade), with a continuous quality-uplift programme advancing it toward A+ alongside the Build Phase.

## What we ask of the Ministry

Section 8 of the report lists six action items across pre-fieldwork, during-fieldwork, and post-field categories. The two pre-fieldwork items are time-sensitive:

1. Confirmation of the field-survey commencement date.
2. Authorisation of launch communication to the field-staff cohort.

The full set can be covered in a single working session at the Honourable Commissioner's convenience. We are available for any clarification or briefing the Ministry needs.

## Looking forward

This Status Report is one of several Registry documents the Ministry will receive in 2026. The Updated Baseline Report follows shortly. After fieldwork concludes, the Field Survey Report will deliver the empirical labour-market findings, together with policy recommendations.

We see the Registry as a living State asset that will support the Ministry's mandate over the long term, and we are at the Ministry's service throughout the engagement.
