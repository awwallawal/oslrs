RE: SUBMISSION OF THE PRE-FIELD SURVEY STATUS REPORT — OYO STATE LABOUR & SKILLS REGISTRY

Honourable Commissioner,

It is our honour and privilege to submit, on behalf of Chemiroy Nigeria Limited, the Pre-Field Survey Status Report for the Oyo State Labour & Skills Registry (OSLSR), prepared in fulfilment of the obligations arising from the Oyo State Business Enabling Reforms Action Plan (BERAP) Year 2025 — Improving access to skilled and formal labour by corporate organisations and businesses through the establishment of the State Electronic Labour Register.

This Report provides the Ministry with a comprehensive account of progress recorded against the seven Action Steps prescribed in the said BERAP plan; the field-survey readiness posture of the Registry platform; and the Ministry-side actions required to authorise field-survey commencement.

We are pleased to confirm the following position as at the date of this Letter:

  1. The Registry platform has been established and equipped to international standards (BERAP Action Steps 3 and 4);
  2. The Labour Data Centre is operational and secured through identity-protected operator access, edge-layer threat protection, and immutable audit logging compliant with the Nigeria Data Protection Act, 2023;
  3. Two of six items in the Field Readiness Certificate have been completed; the remaining four are in active development with completion targeted before the field-survey commencement window opens;
  4. Capacity-building materials for the field staff (thirty-three Supervisors and ninety-nine Enumerators across the thirty-three Local Government Areas of the State) are in production for delivery in the days immediately preceding fieldwork;
  5. The Build–Operate–Transfer model governing this engagement is summarised at Section 7 of the Report; the Transfer Protocol is drafted and awaits the Honourable Commissioner’s counter-signature.

The Report sets out, in Section 8, a small number of Ministry Action Items requiring the Ministry’s attention to authorise fieldwork and progress the engagement to its next phase. None of these Items requires complex inter-Ministerial coordination, and all may be addressed in a single working session at the Honourable Commissioner’s convenience.

We respectfully solicit the Ministry’s confirmation of the field-survey commencement schedule and the related authorisations described in the Report.

We remain, at all times, at the service of the Honourable Commissioner and the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Cooperatives for any clarification, briefing, or additional information that may be required in furtherance of this engagement.

Please accept, Honourable Commissioner, the renewed assurance of our highest consideration.

Yours faithfully,

 

 

Lawal Awwal Principal Consultant Chemiroy Nigeria Limited

 

Mrs Fateemah Roy-Lagbaja Managing Director Chemiroy Nigeria Limited
