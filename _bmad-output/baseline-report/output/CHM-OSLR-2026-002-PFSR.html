2. Mandate Audit — Progress Against BERAP Action Steps

Private-sector partners on record include the Manufacturing Association of Nigeria (MAN), the Association of Nigerian Artisans and Technicians (ASNAT), and the Association of Skilled and Vocational Artisans of Nigeria (ASVAN).

2.1 Stakeholder engagement (Action Step 1)

Engagement with the private-sector contributors named in the BERAP plan continues. The Registry’s Marketplace tier (anonymous public search interface for skilled labour) is the operational artefact through which Manufacturing Association of Nigeria (MAN), Association of Nigerian Artisans and Technicians (ASNAT), and Association of Skilled and Vocational Artisans of Nigeria (ASVAN) member organisations will access skilled-labour profiles after the field survey populates the Register.

Federal-level positioning is also acknowledged: the Skilled-Up Nigeria Programme (SUPA) launched by the Federal Ministry under the Industrial Training Fund (ITF) in March 2026 targets ten million artisans nationally. The Oyo State Labour & Skills Registry is positioned as a complementary state-level layer that adds LGA-granular validation, fraud detection, and identity assurance — value that a national-scale federal programme cannot economically provide. A Ministry decision on formal positioning vis-à-vis SUPA is among the Ministry action items at the end of this Report.

2.2 Engagement of Consultant (Action Step 2) — Complete

Chemiroy Nigeria Limited is the engaged consultant, accountable for the Registry programme through the field-survey deliverable and into the Transfer Phase. The Principal Consultant on record is Lawal Awwal, supported by the Managing Director, Mrs Fateemah Roy-Lagbaja.

2.3 Labour Data Centre — Establishment (Action Step 3) — Complete

The Labour Data Centre operates as a hybrid on-premises plus cloud architecture. Production hosting is on a dedicated DigitalOcean droplet (Ubuntu 24.04 LTS, secured to the standards described later in this Report). Storage is split across:

  • A primary PostgreSQL 15 database for transactional respondent records
  • A Redis 7 in-memory cache for session, queue, and rate-limit state
  • DigitalOcean Spaces (S3-compatible) for daily encrypted backups and respondent media (photographs, identification documents)

Connectivity to the Data Centre is restricted to authorised operator devices through a private overlay network (Tailscale), with key-only Secure Shell access and additional brute-force mitigation (fail2ban) at the operating system layer.

2.4 Labour Data Centre — Equipping (Action Step 4) — Complete

The Registry platform is deployed and operational across five user roles (Public, Enumerator, Clerk, Supervisor, Assessor, Super Admin):

  • Front-end web application (React, optimised for mobile devices; Progressive Web App with seven-day offline draft capability for field operations under intermittent connectivity)
  • API service (Express on Node.js, rate-limited per endpoint, security headers enforced at the application and edge layers)
  • Native form system with skip logic, validation rules, and ILO-aligned occupation taxonomy mapped to the International Standard Classification of Occupations (ISCO-08)
  • Fraud Signal Engine with three configurable heuristics (geographic clustering, speed-run detection, response-pattern analysis)
  • Audit trail subsystem with append-only, hash-chained logs supporting the seven-year retention period required by the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA)
  • Analytics infrastructure including descriptive statistics, inferential statistics (chi-square, correlations, group comparisons with 95% confidence intervals), cross-tabulation, equity analysis, geographic visualisation (LGA choropleth), and PDF policy-brief export — designed and validated against a synthetic dataset; ready to interpret field-survey data without further engineering

A registered super-administrator account is operational, supplemented by a break-glass second super-administrator account for continuity-of-operations.

2.5 Capacity Building (Action Step 5) — In Progress

The capacity-building programme covers four modules: Enumerator, Supervisor, Administrator, and Government Officials. Curriculum content is drafted; final training materials including the Operations Manual (enumerator section) and Supervisor field-protocol document are in active production. Delivery to the field-staff cohort is scheduled to occur in the days immediately preceding fieldwork commencement.

2.6 Handover to Ministry (Action Step 6) — Designed

The Build-Operate-Transfer model governing this engagement is summarised in the Build-Operate-Transfer Framing section of this Report. The formal Transfer Protocol is drafted. The Honourable Commissioner has indicated willingness to counter-sign the Transfer Protocol; final scheduling of the counter-signature appointment is among the Ministry action items at the end of this Report.

2.7 Monitoring and Evaluation (Action Step 7) — Designed and Operational

A Key Performance Indicator framework of thirty-four indicators across five domains — Enumeration Progress, Data Quality, Field Operations, Platform Performance, and Registry Outcomes — provides the canonical measurement structure for the Registry. System-health monitoring is operational at the platform level, with automated alerts on infrastructure metrics. Monitoring continues throughout the field-survey window and into the Operate Phase.

3. Platform Capabilities and Posture

This section sets out the substantive capabilities of the Registry as deployed and the security posture under which it operates.

3.1 Registry capabilities

The Registry platform supports the following capability set:

  • Analytics tier: descriptive statistics, inferential statistics (chi-square, correlations, group comparisons with 95% confidence intervals), cross-tabulation, equity analysis, geographic visualisation (Local Government Area choropleth), anonymised public insights, and policy-brief PDF export
  • Pending-NIN deferred-capture model: a defined follow-up cadence (T+2 days, +7 days, +14 days, +30 days) for respondents whose National Identification Number is not available at enumeration, allowing enumeration to proceed without delay
  • Multi-source registry framework: ingestion of secondary data sources (PDF, CSV, XLSX) with dry-run preview, confirmation, and a fourteen-day rollback window — relevant to coordination with federal SUPA data and historical Ministry datasets
  • Public Wizard: a single five-step self-registration flow with email magic-link primary authentication, designed to minimise public-registration friction
  • Administrative audit-log viewer: investigation-grade access to platform activity at scale, supporting Super-Admin oversight

3.2 Security posture

The Registry’s security posture is independently assessed at B+ grade, with a defined path to A- on the threat-model dimensions appropriate for a state-government registry handling personally identifying information:

  • Identity and operator access: Tailscale private overlay network for administrator access; key-only Secure Shell; fail2ban brute-force mitigation; full sshd hardening across configuration drop-ins
  • Edge protection: Cloudflare-managed Web Application Firewall (WAF) and Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) mitigation in front of the public Registry domain
  • Application-layer security: Content Security Policy mirrored between application and edge with automated drift-detection testing; multi-origin Cross-Origin Resource Sharing parsing for the dual-domain configuration
  • Data integrity: Append-only, hash-chained audit logs; advisory-lock concurrency control on critical writes; deduplicated National Identification Number constraint enforcement

3.3 Domain and communications infrastructure

The Registry’s public-facing infrastructure is configured for a multi-domain deployment with verified outbound transactional email through Resend, configured against the DKIM, Sender Policy Framework, and Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting and Conformance (DMARC) standards. Inbound contact addresses (administrator, support, information) are configured through a dedicated forwarding service. Day-to-day Ministry communications can be routed to designated addresses without further engineering work.

6. Updated Compliance Posture

6.1 Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023

The Registry’s compliance posture is NDPA-aligned, with Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) drafted and filing with the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC) planned for the second quarter of 2026.

The substantive controls supporting this posture are: collection of National Identification Number as the sole personally-identifying identifier; seven-year retention; immutable audit logs; encrypted-at-rest backups (with client-side encryption being added on top); and role-based access control. These controls are described in greater detail in the security and platform sections of this Report.

6.2 Lawful Basis

The lawful basis for processing is recorded in the DPIA as public task in pursuit of an official function of the Oyo State Government, supported by individual respondent consent recorded at enumeration through the platform’s two-stage consent workflow. National Identification Number is the sole personally-identifying identifier collected; Bank Verification Numbers are explicitly not collected. Marketplace-tier disclosure to private-sector searchers is gated by separate respondent consent recorded as a distinct decision in the platform.

6.3 Data Sharing

A formal Data Sharing Agreement template has been drafted to govern any future provision of Registry data to third parties (federal initiatives, research partners, or private-sector consumers under the Marketplace tier). The template is in legal review.

7. Build → Operate → Transfer Framing

The Oyo State Labour & Skills Registry is governed by a Build-Operate-Transfer (BOT) model. This framing was implicit in the BERAP Action Steps but is explicitly stated in this Report to support the Ministry’s understanding of the present and forward-looking responsibility allocation.

7.1 Build Phase

The Build Phase is the platform construction and field-survey period. It is the current phase. Chemiroy Nigeria Limited holds the consultant accountability for delivery and operates the platform on behalf of the Ministry through this period.

7.2 Operate Phase

The Operate Phase begins on the day field-survey activities are concluded and the Registry transitions from “instrument under validation” to “production register of skilled labour for the State.” During the Operate Phase, Chemiroy Nigeria Limited continues to provide platform hosting, administrative support, and quarterly reporting. The Ministry’s substantive use of the Registry — for workforce planning, skills programmes, and private-sector engagement — begins in this phase.

7.3 Transfer Phase

The Transfer Phase culminates in formal handover of the Registry to Ministry custodianship. The Transfer Protocol drafted for this engagement covers:

  • Source code and intellectual property licensing
  • Domain ownership and migration of supporting service accounts
  • Database custody and operational handover
  • Capacity transfer to Ministry information technology personnel
  • Legal indemnification and post-Transfer support arrangement

The Transfer Protocol is in legal review and will be presented to the Honourable Commissioner for counter-signature on a date to be confirmed by the Ministry.

Key finding

The Build-Operate-Transfer model concentrates the Ministry's substantive obligation in the Operate Phase (substantive use of the Registry) and the Transfer Phase (custodianship). The Build Phase, including this field-survey activity, is delivered under consultant accountability. The Ministry's strategic decisions in this period concern field-survey authorisation, post-field stakeholder engagement programming, and Transfer-Day scheduling.
