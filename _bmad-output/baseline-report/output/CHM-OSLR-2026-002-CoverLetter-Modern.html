Honourable Commissioner,

This letter accompanies the Pre-Field Survey Status Report for the Oyo State Labour & Skills Registry (OSLSR). The Report summarises the state of the Registry programme in advance of fieldwork, scheduled to commence in the first week of May 2026.

Where the programme stands

The Registry has progressed to a deployed, secured, and operational state. Specifically:

  • The Registry is established and operational. The platform supports five user roles (Public, Enumerator, Clerk, Supervisor, Assessor, and Super Admin), offline-first operation for field conditions, and a four-layer data-quality assurance protocol.
  • The data centre is secured and equipped. Operator access is restricted to a private overlay network (Tailscale), public traffic is protected by an enterprise edge layer (Cloudflare WAF and DDoS mitigation), and audit logs are append-only and hash-chained for the seven-year retention required by the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023.
  • The analytics layer is in place. The Registry can produce descriptive statistics, inferential statistics, cross-tabulations, equity analyses, geographic visualisations, and PDF policy briefs against field-survey data without further engineering work.
  • The Field Readiness Certificate stands at 2 of 6 complete. The remaining four items are in active development with completion targeted before the field-survey window opens. None depends on Ministry input.

What we ask of the Ministry

Section 8 of the Report lists eleven action items, organised into pre-fieldwork, during-fieldwork, post-field, and strategic categories. The four pre-fieldwork items are time-sensitive:

  1. Confirmation of the field-survey commencement date
  2. Authorisation of launch communication to the field-staff cohort
  3. (Optional) Designation of a Ministry IT verification window before fieldwork
  4. Designation of a Ministry-side liaison for daily field-progress reporting

The eleven action items together can be addressed in a single working session at the Honourable Commissioner’s convenience. We are at the Ministry’s service for any clarification or briefing required.

Looking forward

This Status Report is one of several Registry-related documents the Ministry will receive in 2026: this Pre-Field Survey Status Report; the Updated Baseline Report; and, post-fieldwork, the Field Survey Report — empirical labour-market findings from the May 2026 fieldwork, with policy recommendations.

We are committed to the Registry as a living State asset that supports the Ministry’s mandate over the long term, and we remain at the Ministry’s service throughout the engagement.
